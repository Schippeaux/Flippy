What is Spaced Repetition?

How it works

Spaced Repetition is a learning technique that shows you information at the exact moment right before you forget it. By spacing out reviews, you strengthen memory and learn efficiently.

Your study process

  1. Study a card: Read English and Spanish
  2. Flip to see answer: Check your understanding
  3. Rate your confidence: ○ (hard) to ● (easy)
  4. System schedules review: Based on your rating
  5. Review when due: At optimal time for memory

Why it works

Research shows that reviewing information at expanding intervals maximizes retention and minimizes study time. Each card is reviewed at the moment you're most likely to forget it, strengthening long-term memory.

Ratings explained

  • ○ (Again): Hard — review immediately
  • ◔ (Hard): Struggled — review soon
  • ◕ (Good): Got it — review in days
  • ● (Easy): Very confident — review in weeks
